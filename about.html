My Story

I first learned the basics of HTML and CSS as a Sophomore in High School. That was the first time I ever programmed anything outside of the scracth website with block code. Only starting in 2020, I took an AP Computer Science Principals class as a Junior, and fell in love with programming. My dad had always been a computer person and we always did projects with computers whenever we got time to. I knew at some point between middle school and high school that I wanted to follow in his footsteps and go into computer science. Since then I have been coding as much as possible and doing as much research as possible, taking all of the opportunities that I can get, and that comes to where I am now.

Where I am now

As of February 2022, I am a 16 year old JavaScript/HTML/CSS tutor for a high school of 3000 active students, getting into some other languages like Python and Java, experimenting with frameworks like selenium and React.js. I am always building my portfolio, and always looking for new opportunities to improve my skills.

Update: As of July 2023, I am an 18 year old Python programmer that works primarily on projects focused on automation and automotive-related technologies. I work well with selenium and other automated systems, and I am always eager to learn.

My goals

My goals for my life change rapidly thoughout my exposure to the real world of computer science. Though this is true, one thing stands clear and constant; I want to be able to make a positive impact on the world. I simply want to be able to make lives better while following in what I am most passionate about. My current "dream career" is to be a software engineer for automotive vehicles and to create performance technology systems or innovate on-board computer systems inside of automotive vehicles.
